Accent classification has been a longstanding problem in artificial intelligence, since parameters like cepstrum coefficients are often unable to take into account the nuances of human speech in terms of features. For this project, internet radio stations and podcasts from all around the world were (legally) scraped during talk shows etc for 23 different spoken English accents. The mined data was then divided into 20-second slices and a neural network implemented in Mathematica was trained on the labeled data in the ratio 50:20:30 for training, validation and testing respectively. The final program showed an accuracy of 87%, which is far greater than previous attempts.

The project was made in July 2015 at the Wolfram Tech Innovation Summer Camp and was fervently lauded.
